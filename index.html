Background

Decisions,
not commentary

Most petrochemical, refining and energy strategy gets written by people who've only ever seen one side of the chain — the trading desk, the plant, or the boardroom. I've worked all three: hedging US ethane cargoes into Reliance's import terminal, building the FP&A models that justified the capital, and now running full P&L for a national distribution business. That's the lens this research is written through — decisions with real capital and real counterparties attached, not market commentary.

I default to MECE breakdowns and SCQA framing to turn ambiguous industry questions into a structured set of options before committing to a recommendation — the same disciplined approach this entire research portfolio is built around.

My work sits at the convergence of commercial strategy, commodity economics, and energy transition, bringing engineering-level process understanding together with the financial and geopolitical lens of an industry practitioner.

The research on this site is written for strategic decision-makers across the US and Gulf energy ecosystem — addressing the capital allocation questions shaping the sector over the next decade.
